9 What is maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) and what is the distinction between the unknown parameter theta and its estimate theta-hat? Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) is a principle that seeks to choose the value of an unknown parameter, $\theta$, which maximizes the probability of observing the actual data collected. The function representing this probability, $P_{D|\theta}$, is called the likelihood of the data $D$. In MLE, one chooses $\theta$ such that the likelihood function, $L_{\theta|D} = P_{D|\theta}$, is maximized with respect to $\theta$. The unknown parameter(s) are denoted by $\theta$, while its estimate derived from this process is written as $\hat{\theta}$.
